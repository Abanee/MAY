How We Began

Our Story

Hover — or tap — each book to open it and read the chapter of our story it holds.

1991
Ch. 1

The Beginning

Josephine Marlowe sold her first hundred books from a market stall, lending out the ones she loved most and asking only that readers bring them back.

1994
Ch. 2

Opening Day

On Birchwood Lane, the doors opened to a single wooden room, seven shelves, and a hand-painted sign: books to keep, and books to borrow.

1999
Ch. 3

Growing Collection

Adrian Finch joined as co-founder, and the collection tripled — mystery, poetry, and a children's corner built from an old ship's cabinet.

2006
Ch. 4

Community Expansion

The lounge next door was knocked through, and the library began lending library cards to the whole neighborhood, free of charge.

2014
Ch. 5

Reading Events

The first Sunday Poetry Circle drew six people and a pot of tea. It has not missed a Sunday since.

2026
Ch. 6

Modern Digital Library

A personal dashboard now sits beside the paper ledger — same handshake, same trust, just easier to track your due dates.

What We Stand For

Mission & Values

Every rule on our shelves — what we buy, what we lend, who we welcome — traces back to six commitments we made on opening day and have never renegotiated.

I.

Inspire Reading

We hand-sell books the way a friend would — by conversation, not algorithm.

II.

Build Community

This room belongs to the block as much as it belongs to us.

III.

Encourage Lifelong Learning

No reader is ever too old, or too new, to start a shelf of their own.

IV.

Support Local Authors

A quarter of our new-arrivals table is reserved for writers from within 30 miles.

V.

Preserve Knowledge

Our rare book section exists so certain editions never quietly disappear.

VI.

Welcome Every Reader

Whatever you're reading, whoever you are — there's a chair here for you.

Meet Our Team

Founder

"A good library remembers you before you remember it."

Opened the shop from a market stall in 1991. Favorite genre: literary fiction. Favorite book: Middlemarch. 35 years in books.

AF

Adrian Finch

Head Librarian

"Cataloguing is just remembering, written down."

Joined in 1999 and built the rare book section by hand. Favorite genre: history. Favorite book: The Name of the Rose. 27 years in libraries.
