How We Began

Our Story & Timeline

From a modest market stall in 1991 to a modern sanctuary for 18,400+ readers — explore thirty years of literary history.

1991 Chapter 01

The Beginning

Josephine Marlowe sold her first hundred books from a market stall, lending out the ones she loved most and asking only that readers bring them back.

1994 Chapter 02

Opening Day

On Birchwood Lane, the doors opened to a single wooden room, seven shelves, and a hand-painted sign: books to keep, and books to borrow.

1999 Chapter 03

Growing Collection

Adrian Finch joined as co-founder, and the collection tripled — mystery, poetry, and a children's corner built from an old ship's cabinet.

2006 Chapter 04

Community Expansion

The lounge next door was knocked through, and the library began lending library cards to the whole neighborhood, free of charge.

2014 Chapter 05

Reading Events

The first Sunday Poetry Circle drew six people and a pot of tea. It has not missed a single Sunday since.

2026 Chapter 06

Modern Digital Library

A personal member dashboard now sits beside the paper ledger — same handshake, same trust, just easier to track your due dates.

Two Ways to Read

Our Shop & Lending Library

From the very first market stall, Marlowe & Finch has carried two promises — to sell books that earn their place on your shelf forever, and to lend titles to every reader, regardless of budget. The ground floor is the bookshop. The reading rooms above are the library. Same address. Two experiences.

Josephine & Adrian · Birchwood Market, 1991

The Original Promise

Books to Keep, & Books to Borrow

When Josephine Marlowe set up her first stall in 1991, she painted two signs. One read For Sale — the books she'd sourced and curated. The other read Borrow & Return — the titles she loved most and couldn't bear to sell. That dual promise opened the very first day of Marlowe & Finch, and has never changed.

Today, the ground floor is home to our curated book shop — new releases, collector's editions, local authors, and a coffee nook. The floors above hold the lending library — 18,400 titles available to every member, tracked by our digital dashboard, with return deadlines that are gentle and fair.
