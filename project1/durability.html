Molecular Engineering

The Physics of Paint protection

Car paint is naturally porous and soft (typically rating between 2H to 4H on the Mohs mineral scale). Washing, environmental pollution, and highway debris slowly score the clear coat, creating micro-abrasions that dull the paint finish over time.

Modern detailing offers two distinct shield mechanisms: **nano-ceramic molecular crystallization** (liquid glass that hardens and fills pores) and **thermoplastic polyurethane wrapping** (an elastic elastomer wrap that acts as a physical shock-absorber). Compare their performance envelopes to match your driving habits.
