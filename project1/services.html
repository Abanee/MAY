Paint Protection Film (PPF) is a thermoplastic urethane film that physically absorbs and deflects rock chips, road debris, minor abrasions and environmental damage. Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does ceramic coating last?
With AURA's Elite package using Ceramic Pro 9H, the coating lasts a minimum of 3 years under the Ceramic Pro warranty. Our Lifetime package uses Ceramic Pro Platinum, which carries a lifetime warranty as long as annual inspections are maintained. Longevity also depends on maintenance — properly washed coated vehicles regularly outperform their warranty by years.
Can ceramic coating prevent rock chips?
No. Ceramic coating will not stop rock chips or deep impact damage — that is the role of Paint Protection Film (PPF). Ceramic adds hardness, chemical resistance, and UV protection, while PPF provides physical impact absorption. For complete protection, AURA recommends a ceramic coating applied over PPF — this is the ultimate combination for long-term paint preservation.
How long does PPF installation take?
A front impact package (hood, bumper, mirrors, headlights) typically takes 1–2 days. A full body wrap on a standard luxury vehicle takes 3–5 days. More complex body shapes — such as exotics with dramatic curves, sills, or splitters — may require additional time. All timelines are confirmed in your written quote.
Do you work at my home or office?
Yes. AURA is a 100% mobile service. We bring our professional setup — including power, water, lighting, and controlled environment enclosures where needed — to your home, office, private garage, or estate. No need to drop your vehicle at a detailing shop. We service a 60-mile radius and can arrange further for exceptional vehicles.
Is window tinting legal?
Laws vary by state and country. In most US states, rear side windows and rear windows can be tinted to 35% VLT or darker without issue. Front side windows are typically restricted to 35-50% VLT. We are fully familiar with local regulations and will only install legal shades to your specified front windows — darker shades are available for rear windows where legally permitted.
What maintenance does a ceramic coating need?
Ceramic coated vehicles should be washed every 2–3 weeks using pH-neutral shampoo and the two-bucket method or a foam cannon. Avoid automatic car washes with brushes. Do not use wax or sealant products on top of the coating. An annual SiO₂ ceramic boost spray refreshes the hydrophobic performance. AURA's maintenance program handles all of this for you.
Can you apply coating to a new vehicle?
Absolutely — and this is the ideal time. New vehicles still benefit from a decontamination wash and light paint prep since factory paint is not defect-free. Applying ceramic or PPF to a new vehicle from day one means the paint is protected before any damage can occur, maximising the vehicle's long-term condition and resale value.
Does paint correction damage my paint?
When performed by trained professionals, paint correction is completely safe. AURA uses paint depth gauges on every panel to ensure sufficient clear coat thickness before polishing. We never remove more than is necessary, and all work is performed with professional-grade machine polishers — not aggressive rotaries in untrained hands. The result is safer and more precise than any automated car wash.
How much does a full detail cost?
A comprehensive package combining paint correction, full body PPF, ceramic coating, and window tint typically ranges from $8,500 to $18,000 depending on vehicle size, body complexity, and chosen products. Standard sedans start at the lower end; exotic sports cars and large SUVs are priced higher. AURA provides transparent, itemised quotes — contact us for a precise estimate for your specific vehicle.
What is your service area?
AURA's primary service area is within a 60-mile radius of our base. For exceptional vehicles, rare exotics, or private collection commissions, we will travel further — nationally or internationally if required. Please contact us to discuss travel arrangements for out-of-area vehicles.
