Defect Profile: Moderate Surface Scratches

Swirl Marks & Spiderweb Scratching

Created by improper hand washing, mechanical brushes, or rough wiping. These micro-scratches act as tiny concave mirrors, scattering light rays in multiple directions instead of reflecting them parallel, destroying gloss depth.

AURA Correction Action: A two-stage paint correction. First, rotary heavy compounding cuts down clear coat surrounding scratches to level surface. Second, dual-action jewel polish refines paint haze to deliver a mirror finish.

Material Engineering

Advanced Material Science

Under the microscope: explore the nanotechnology and polymer chemistry powering the protective performance of AURA treatments.

Covalent Nanostructure
Ceramic Pro 9H

Silicon dioxide (SiO₂) nanopolymer chains form high-density covalent cross-links with factory clear coats. Increases surface hardness above 9H on the Mohs scale, preventing fine contact abrasions and locking in permanent gloss depth.

Binding Type:Covalent Molecular
Hardness:>9H Mohs Scale
Layer Thickness:2.0 microns / coat
Elastomeric Polymer
XPEL Self-Healing

Constructed with a highly flexible thermoplastic polyurethane (TPU) base. Features shape-memory elastomeric polymers that self-heal swirl marks and light road debris impacts when heated by direct sunlight or engine heat.

Material Base:Aliphatic Polyurethane
Elongation:Up to 400% stretch
Healing Trigger:Direct Heat (80°F+)
Spectral Filtration
Infrared Ceramic

Nano-ceramic particles suspended in optical-grade polyester block infrared solar radiation. Rejects up to 96% of cabin-heating wavelengths while maintaining absolute transparency and zero signal interference for devices.

IR Rejection:96% (900-1000nm)
UV Block:99.9% SPF 1000+
Material Spec:Antimony Tin Oxide
